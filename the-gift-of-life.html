Our purpose is to get lives together and to provide the opportunity for those with more in life and of life, to share with those with less.

The Gift of L.I.F.E.

There are many ways that L.I.F.E. gives its children some hope for life:

Adoption A benefactor "clinically adopts" a patient by paying for his chemotherapy, blood transfusions and infection control regimen for the crucial 3-year treatment period. Treatment costs roughly P380,000. You may provide:

- Full Coverage - cost of full 3-year treatment - Limited - cost for 1 year* or - Partial - cost for 1 semester*

Adoptions may come from corporations or individuals. A group of friends may co-adopt a child and form a "Circle of L.I.F.E."

Support Programs L.I.F.E. places as much importance on addressing the emotional, psycho-social and spiritual needs of its sick children. It holds training programs to professionalize the delivery of support services, including modules on counseling, advocacy, and volunteer training.

The support group is composed mostly of parents and friends of the patients. On a daily basis, a "Mother of the Day" is designated to listen and lend comfort to the patients of the day.

Art, Music, Play and Spiritual Therapy Artists lend their talent by giving lessons for leukemic children at the clinic. As they await their chemotherapy, the children are encouraged to engage in creative work. Monthly healing Masses are held for their families.

Fellowship Regular events are held exclusively for the children. These include quarterly birthday celebrations, annual Foundation Day and Christmas parties. Every last Sunday of April finds the children and their families engaged in a mini-Olympics event dubbed "Sports for L.I.F.E.".
